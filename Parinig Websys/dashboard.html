Shattered Silence

Posted on: April 28, 2025, 10:30 am

I was only seventeen when it happened. It was supposed to be a normal night — a birthday celebration with friends I trusted, in a house I’d been to a dozen times before. I laughed. I danced. I drank something from a red plastic cup handed to me by someone I thought I knew. What happened after is blurry in fragments, like broken glass scattered on a dark floor. I remember saying “no”. I remember the pressure. The weight. The shame. And then... silence. The morning after, I woke up feeling like my body didn’t belong to me anymore. I stared at the ceiling for hours, feeling numb and dirty. I told no one for weeks. I blamed myself — for going out, for drinking, for trusting. But deep inside, I knew: I didn't do this to myself. Someone else did. When I finally told someone — my older sister — she didn’t ask what I was wearing. She didn’t question why I waited. She just believed me. That moment saved me more than I’ll ever be able to explain. The road to healing wasn’t easy. Therapy helped. Writing helped. Some days, I still feel like I’m putting myself back together piece by piece. But I’m not broken anymore — I’m rebuilding. And I’m telling this story, not because it’s easy, but because silence protects no one but the abuser. I want others to know: It wasn’t your fault. Your voice matters. And healing is possible.
